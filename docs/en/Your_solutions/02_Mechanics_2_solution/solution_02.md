# 🎵 Harmonic Motion — Spring Mass System

## 📘 Problem

A 10 kg mass oscillates according to:

$$
x(t) = 0.2\cos(10\pi t)
$$

where displacement is in **meters**.

Find:

1. Spring constant \(k\)  
2. Total mechanical energy of the system  

---

# 🧠 Step 1 — Compare with SHM Standard Equation

Standard simple harmonic motion:

$$
x(t) = A\cos(\omega t)
$$

Where:

- \(A\) = amplitude  
- \(\omega\) = angular frequency  
- \(m\) = mass  
- \(k\) = spring constant  

Comparing:

$$
x(t) = 0.2\cos(10\pi t)
$$

We identify:

Amplitude:

$$
A = 0.2 \text{ m}
$$

Angular frequency:

$$
\omega = 10\pi
$$

Mass:

$$
m = 10 \text{ kg}
$$

---

# 🚀 Step 2 — Use SHM Formula

For spring motion:

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

Solve for \(k\):

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

---

# 🧮 Step 3 — Substitute Values

$$
k = 10(10\pi)^2
$$

$$
k = 10(100\pi^2)
$$

$$
k = 1000\pi^2
$$

$$
k \approx 1000(9.87)
$$

$$
k \approx 9870 \text{ N/m}
$$

---

# ✅ Spring Constant

$$
k \approx 9.87 \times 10^3 \text{ N/m}
$$

---

# ⚡ Step 4 — Total Mechanical Energy

Total energy of SHM:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

---

# 🧮 Step 5 — Substitute

Amplitude:

$$
A = 0.2
$$

Spring constant:

$$
k = 9870
$$

$$
E = \frac12 (9870)(0.2)^2
$$

$$
E = 0.5(9870)(0.04)
$$

$$
E = 0.5(394.8)
$$

$$
E = 197.4 \text{ J}
$$

---

# ✅ Total Mechanical Energy

$$
E \approx 197 \text{ J}
$$

---

# 🏁 Final Answers

### Spring Constant

$$
k \approx 9.87 \times 10^3 \text{ N/m}
$$

### Total Mechanical Energy

$$
E \approx 197 \text{ J}
$$

---
